Winter 2018 Spain Trip

Image highlights

❮ ❯

Trip Planning/Notes

  • Hotel: Gran Melia Colon - one of the best hotels I have ever stayed in: free buffet breakfast with so many delicious options (including lox, iberian ham, brie cheese, churros...), free flamenco dancing shows every Saturday evening, and truly unparalleled service and accomodations!

  • Transportation: Everything within Seville is walking distance and just enjoying the leisurely strolls truly allows you to feel the delightful ambience of old Spanish architecture and blooming orange trees to their fullest.


Trip Diary

  • Day 1 - Seville

    • Catedral de Sevilla

      Visited this magnificent cathedral with some truly stunning cieling designs, portrait rooms, and gold crowns.

      • Would probably be a good idea to book tickets ahead of time as we didn't and had to wait about 30-45 min just to get in.

      • Make sure to request a separate tour to visit the tower (La Giralda) as you can enjoy the best views of the city and the interesting designs along the cathedral roofs.

    • Real Alcazar de Sevilla

      This place reminds me of a summer palace (which it probably is) with some very unique architecture, beautiful ceilings, and a large, beautiful garden full of fountains and mini mazes.

      • Also a good idea to book tickets ahead of time as we didn't and had to wait about 45 min to an hour just to get in.

      • Keep an eye out for the peacock that casually roams around and poses!

    • Christopher Columbus Monument

      Seemed quite close to the Real Alcazar, but due to the gates, it was actually quite a walk. Probably the least exciting landmark we visited but still cool!

    • Plaza de Espana

      Beautiful plaza with horses, fountains, boats, and live flamenco dancers that create a relaxing and delightful environment. It's a truly magnificent area with many people milling around and beautiful architecture as befits a place to hold the World Expo.

    • Universidad de Sevilla

      Walked through the university on our way back from Plaza de Espana to downtown Seville because why not?

    • Amorino

      Wandered through the streets looking for a quick dinner and ended up having a very bad meal at Don Carlo, so of course, we decided to then fill ourselves up with ice cream and had some of the best ice cream ever at Amorino! Truly was so good that we came back the next day! Ordered the pistachio, vanilla, and coffee (3 scoops) with a vanilla macaron and it was the best thing ever! Also tried the black cherry sundae which was really good too.

  • Day 2 - Granada

    • Granada

      See my Granada post for details on how we visited Granada! Would definitely recommend making a half-day trip there while visiting Seville.

    • Catunambu

      Enjoyed some churros dipped in hot chocolate for dinner (yes, I know but it's vacation) and then topped that off with more ice cream at Amorino nearby! To be honest, the churros weren't the best we'd ever tried but they were decent--would probably try a different place next time though.

    • Flamenco Dancing

      Finished the day with some flamenco dancing at our hotel in Gran Melia Colon. Also ordered drinks and a fruit platter (that was actually quite large!) to go with it.


Other places I hope to visit next time

  • Uno de Delicias - should have come here for dinner the first night, but didn't. Seems like a cute place with many great reviews!
