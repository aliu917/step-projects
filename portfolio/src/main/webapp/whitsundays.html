Trip Planning/Notes

We stayed on Hamilton Island, which is a privately-owned island in the Whitsundays with its own entire little community. Everything on the island costs a little more than the equivalent in Airie Beach or mainland towns but you pay a premium for the convenience, hospitality, and island resort environment, which are unparalleled. Wild baby kangaroo run freely around, mango trees and beautiful tropical plants populate the surroundings, and everyone we met was incredibly freindly. Would highly recommend staying here for the ultimate Whitsundays experience!

  • In high peak seasons (like Christmas/New Years when we visited) book months early or else there will be no availabilities!

  • Hotel: Reef View Hotel - all rooms have an amazing ocean view balcony and every morning we would wake up to white cockatoos along the railing.

  • Check out the Hamilton Island website for island activities and reserve early! (You can cancel reservations up to 2 days before.)

  • Half of the restaurants on the island require reservations and the most popular is Coca Chu (everybody recommended it but there were no availabilities for the next 2 months!)

  • Make sure to bring an wall outlet that fits in the Australian wall plug. (We didn't have one and were charging our phones on our laptops the entire time.)


Trip Diary

  • Day 1 - Travel Half Day

    • Catseye Beach

      Checked into our hotel and headed straight to the beach which was only a 3 min walk from our hotel.

    • Manta Ray

      Delicious food with a beautiful view of the harbor. We ordered the tuna tataki, torched salmon sushi tacos, prawn linguini, and salmon fillet--all of which were amazing!

    • Australia the Gift

      Cute little gift shop located right by the Reef View Hotel where we bought a couple little local trinkets.

  • Day 2 - Heart Reef and Whitehaven Beach

    • Bob Bakery

      Woke up early to take a quick stroll along the marina and grabbed some pastries for breakfast. Our favorite was the fruit tarts which we ended up getting for breakfast every morning after.

    • Heart Reef Helicopter tour

      It had rained all morning and weather forecast warned of lightning but luckily, the weather was just starting to clear for our helicopter tour at 9 (still had a couple scattered showers though).

      • We took the Heart Reef and Whitehaven Scenic

        helicopter tour which was 1 hour long and flew over all the major areas like Whitehaven beach, hill inlet, heart reef, and surrounding island areas.

      • To be honest, the one-hour helicopter ride was a bit long and most of it (about 30-35 min) was spent just traveling to and from the heart reef with and wasn't as exciting as seeing the major attractions. Granted, the heart reef is definitely worth visitng, but for us, the area above the heart reef was still quite stormy so we didn't get the nice picturesque sunny view but it was still cool!

      • Personally, my favorite was hill inlet and seeing the beautiful sand spirals so if I came back, I would probably stick with the shorter Whitehaven Photo Scenic which just covers Whitehaven Beach, hill inlet, and the nearby islands.

      • It also might be worth checking out Reefworld Fly/Cruise which has all the benefits of visiting all the major helicopter tour attractions but 20 min less because there is no helicopter return trip. You would additionally get to snorkel out at Reefworld among the coral reefs and return by cruise. However, this option was fully booked when I tried to reserve 2 months in advance and it also didn't fit into our schedule since we only had one full day and wanted to visit Whitehaven Beach in the afternoon.

      • Overall though, would definitely recommend a helicopter tour of some kind because the pictures from above are absolutely stunning!

    • Marina Cafe

      Stopped by for a quick snack and just ordered a bunch of sushi rolls and a mango tropical smoothie. Smoothie was good but not spectacular. The sushi was the perfect light lunch meal and we ended up taking them to-go the next day for lunch too!

    • Whitehaven Beach

      Declared as the second best beach in the world, Whitehaven Beach was the perfect place to spend a sunny afternoon (it had luckily stopped raining by then). The sand is unbelievably white and soft, and we all spent most of the afternoon just floating in the clear ocean water. We took the Cruise Whitesundays Whitehaven Beach Half-day afternoon tour and it was perfect.

      • Incredibly clean and pristine beach that was not overcrowded (as there are only a handful of cruises that stop there each afternoon). The soft white sand was definitely the highlight followed closely by the clear blue refreshing waters.

      • Visiting during December, there were invisible poisonous jellyfish in the ocean so Cruise Whitsundays provided everybody with free full-body lycra suits to borrow and they were very clean and easy to slip on.

      • Cruise Whitsundays also provided a free charcuterie platter buffet with surprisingly many options and sodas/drinks as passengers were boarding for the return trip.

    • Tako

      Ate dinner at this Mexican fusion restaurant and tried the Jalapeno poppers, grilled corn on the cob, pulled pork tacos, and seared adobo marinated fish. Food was good and the restaurant had a very energetic vibe but I personally enjoyed Manta Ray more.

  • Day 3 - Travel Half Day

    • We had a flight in the late morning so strolled around the marina once more, got another fruit tart and some sushi rolls to-go, and we were on our way to Melbourne!
